AWS Build On Hackathon 2020

Proposed Fact Checker extension to major chat platforms to combat cybercrimes, infodemics and misinformation.

Problem Statement

“With fake news and the spread of misinformation as one of the defining issues of our time, incorrect, misleading or false advice can travel around the world before anyone has a chance to correct it. From selling fake coronavirus cures online to a cyberattack on hospitals’ critical information systems, criminals are exploiting the COVID-19 crisis.
How can we help to curb misinformation & make tools smarter to combat future infodemics?”

Our Solution

Our proposal is centered around the idea that the best defense for misinformation over the internet is through education, and hence our solution is a tool built to encourage education of the masses on how to be discerning readers, distinguishing itself from the various fact checkers and fake news flags in the market. We proposed an extension to major chat applications such as WhatsApp, covering two key features - an overlay to chat messages with embedded URLs highlighting key contextual information, and a double checking prompt when forwarding long, informative messages.

The contextual information is presented in an overlay on top of the chat message with 4 key metrics - Credibility, Bias, Emotions and Popularity. This serves as an ever-present yet subtle reminder to all readers that the context of how an article is written should always be considered together with its content when determining how trustworthy it is. The other feature slows down users when forwarding long messages typically spread through group chats, engaging the users to perform double checks, using the human’s ability to sieve out fake news as research has shown our strong ability to do so independently. Overall, the solution aims to gradually incorporate contextual information and double checks in the way that we consume information such that the public can be less dependent on tools, and more capable to innately reject misinformation when presented with them.
